## The Hard Truth

<Warning title="Read This Carefully">
Open source contribution is **NOT**:
- A shortcut to getting hired in 3 months
- A way to get quick fame or recognition
- Easy or fast (especially at first)
- Always welcoming or friendly

It **IS**:
- A long-term investment (6-12+ months to see results)
- A learning opportunity unlike any other
- Sometimes frustrating and slow
- Ultimately rewarding for those who persist
</Warning>

## Self-Assessment

Before diving in, honestly answer these questions:

<Checklist>
<ChecklistItem>I can commit 5-10 hours per week for at least 3-6 months</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem>I'm okay with my first few contributions taking weeks to get reviewed</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem>I can handle rejection and use it as a learning opportunity</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem>I'm interested in genuine learning, not just GitHub green squares</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem>I understand maintainers are volunteers with limited time</ChecklistItem>
<ChecklistItem>I'm willing to read documentation thoroughly before asking questions</ChecklistItem>
</Checklist>

**Checked all boxes?** You're ready. Let's begin.

**Missing some?** That's okay—this section will help you develop the right mindset.
