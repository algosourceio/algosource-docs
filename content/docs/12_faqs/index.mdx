# FAQs & Common Failure Patterns

Learn from others' mistakes and get your questions answered.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long until I can call myself a contributor?

After your first merged PR. Seriously. One contribution makes you a contributor.

### How much time do I really need?

Minimum 5 hours/week when starting. You can reduce this once you're familiar with a project.

### What if my PR gets rejected?

It happens to everyone. Ask for feedback, learn, and try again. It's not personal.

### Should I contribute to popular projects?

Popular projects have more support but more competition. Mid-sized active projects are often better for beginners.

### Do I need to know everything before starting?

No. Learning while contributing is normal. Ask questions when stuck.

## Common Failure Patterns

<Warning title="These Kill Most Contributors">
Avoid these patterns that cause most newcomers to fail.
</Warning>

### Pattern 1: Starting Too Big

**Symptom:** Attempting a major feature as first contribution.
**Solution:** Start embarrassingly small. Typo fixes are valid.

### Pattern 2: Disappearing

**Symptom:** Opening a PR and never responding to feedback.
**Solution:** Respond within 48 hours. Communicate if you need time.

### Pattern 3: Arguing with Maintainers

**Symptom:** Fighting every code review suggestion.
**Solution:** Trust maintainers know their codebase. Adapt your approach.

### Pattern 4: No Communication

**Symptom:** Working in silence for weeks then submitting huge PR.
**Solution:** Communicate early and often. Ask questions. Share progress.
